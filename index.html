THE 12 STORIES OF CHRISTMAS

As the year draws to a close, we extend our warmest season's greetings to all our customers, colleagues, collaborators, and friends. It's been another busy, rewarding year at Ballymore, with exciting progress and milestones on both sides of the Irish Sea. Thank you for being a part of our journey as we continue to build vibrant communities and make a lasting impact.

Sean Mulryan

Meeting London's housing needs

We are transforming London's landscape with approximately 10,000 homes now submitted for planning in London at places like Edgware, Ladbroke Grove , Knights Road, Thames Road and Stratford. Meanwhile, Camden Council appointed Ballymore and Lateral to deliver the Camley Street project in the borough.

Planning approvals at Bishopsgate

Working with our partners Hammerson, we progressed the redevelopment of Goodsyard with approval on two Reserved Matters Applications from the Greater London Authority on key elements, including workspace, retail, and the restoration of historic Sclater Street buildings.

Blazing a trail in Brentford

The Brentford Project redefined regeneration in West London, attracting new residents and businesses, including Everyman Cinema, which took 10,973 sq ft space as our anchor leisure tenant this year. We revitalised Workhouse Dock, hosted dynamic community events, including a two-month open air cinema with Everyman, and earned multiple accolades - including a British Homes Award.

Creating a new coastal community for Bray

Residents moved into the first of 1,200 homes at Sea Gardens, our brand-new community located on the picturesque east coast of Ireland in Bray. Sea Gardens offer beautifully designed homes and plans for abundant green spaces, modern amenities, a multi-use games area, and much more.

Recognition for River Walk 

River Walk in Ballymore Eustace, Kildare, was honoured with the prestigious Irish Construction Award - testament to the work we've accomplished in enhancing the existing village. This project has delivered 80 energy-efficient homes in the very place where our journey began 40 years ago.

Celebrating 75 Years of English National Ballet 

English National Ballet (ENB) marked its 75th anniversary, with a special celebration attended by Her Majesty Queen Camilla. Hosted at the Mulryan Centre for Dance on London City Island, the centre's name honours Sean Mulryan and his wife Bernardine in recognition of their profound support for ENB.

Honouring key figures in our neighbourhoods 

Ireland's former President Mary McAleese joined Sean Mulryan at Embassy Gardens, unveiling a new commemorative plaque in honour of Anglo-Irish suffragette Charlotte Despard, a pioneering figure in social activism, suffrage, and humanitarian efforts.

Promoting homegrown trades and craftsmanship

We partnered with Guaranteed Irish, collaborating on the Irish House initiative - reinforcing Ballymore's dedication to supporting Irish businesses and communities, while also strengthening our supply chain.

Engaging events

We hosted a Property Week panel talk in Brentford, joined Sadiq Khan at The London Conference, and attended a Bisnow panel in Ireland. We celebrated urban design with walking tours alongside the London Festival of Architecture and NLA . Meanwhile, our neighbourhoods buzzed with activity, with music, arts and food helping us foster vibrant community connections at Embassy Gardens, The Brentford Project, Goodluck Hope, and Riverscape.

Celebrating Kildare's culinary culture

We sponsored the Taste of Kildare festival this September, a vibrant event that celebrates the region's talented people and flourishing culinary scene, further demonstrating our commitment to Kildare, where we have already built 4,000 homes.

Creating more new homes

We launched two new buildings at Riverscape in London's Royal Docks. and Quincy House - the first offering in Phase 2 at Brentford. In Ireland, we completed Seamount Rise in Malahide, Station Walk, and Stonehaven in Naas. Our first homes at Drumnigh Oaks in Portmarnock, many built to Passive House standards, will soon be available, with additional developments planned at Old Connell, Confey, and Mullingar.

Making a positive social impact

We provided free retail space in Edgware, helped establish the New Edgware Skills Centre, raised £10,000 for Auditory Verbal, and supported World Heart Beat’s Christmas fundraiser. In Ireland, we funded a playground in Malahide, a football pitch in Ballymore Eustace, planted 1,500 trees in Bray, and partnered with Dublin City University to support Gaelic football talent.
